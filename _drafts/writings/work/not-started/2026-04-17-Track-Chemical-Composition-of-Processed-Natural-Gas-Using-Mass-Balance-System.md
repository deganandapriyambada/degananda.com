# Natural gas vs processed natural gas composition

It all started with any living beings (plant, animal) residue (often called as a fossil) that has been perserved on the natural for millions of years and slowly turned into natural gas formation which can be found on rock sendiment/geological formations. These rock can be located on the land as well as on the oceanfloor/seabed.

Human digged those rock to extract the natural gas or crude oil and turned them into hydrocarbons product such as gasoline, liquified petroleum gas (LPG), plastics and so on.

The variaty of hydrocarbon product are so wide due to the rich composition of natural gas which mainly is hydrocarbons (formation of hydrogen and carbon) compound that consist of

1. Methane
2. Alkanes
3. Nitrogen
4. Carbon Dioxide
5. Hydrogen Sulfide
6. Helium

## Mass Balance System to Track Chemical Composition of Natural gas and Processed Natural Gas

The hydrocarbon composition need to be monitored thorough the end to end life cycle of the natural gas processing cycle mainly to ensure the safety and met the product requirement specifications. For example, dry gas consist of 95% methane while LPG only contain very small amount of methane (less than 1%). The process of tracking hydrocarbons composition in context of natural gas processing is called as mass balance method. 

The process of determining hydrocarbon composition is called as gas quality analysis which is done on the lab, while mass balance system used to reconcile hydrocarbon allocation throught the entire value chain ensuring proper allocation, inventory tracking and to detect imbalances for each stakeholder. Mass balances is used to pass industry standard certification such as ISCC and REDCert.

mass balance principle:

	All material entering the system must equal to material leaving the system. It checks the volume and mass of the hydrocarbon.
