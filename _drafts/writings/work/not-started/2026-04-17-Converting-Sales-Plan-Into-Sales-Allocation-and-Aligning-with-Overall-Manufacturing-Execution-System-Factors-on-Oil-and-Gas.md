# Sales Plan is The Basis of Sales Allocation

Every manufacturing industry regardless the equity ownership (private or public) has certain amount of sales target every fiscal year. For example, a tire producer might need to produce 100K pair of tires every year. The process of determining target revenue per fiscal year in done on the sales planning.

Sales plan is not solely based on the stakeholder target but it should cater following aspects:

1. Market trends
2. Historical Sales data
3. Production capacity
4. Human resources capacity
5. Equipment readiness

These factors are different for every organization. For example in oil and gas industry, the sales planning might also be dependent on the weather as the vessel arrival or schedule is dependent on the weather factor.

Sales planning is done prior to the sales allocation. The sales target will be used as basis to determine the actionable item to achieve the sales target. For example: sales department targeting 100K shoes to be sold in FY27 based on market research and internal resource capacity. The action to determine the sales target is called as sales planning. Then, the sales department created a strategy on how to distribute the sales over their customer. Company A will receive 60% and the rest will be evenly distributed to other customer. Those action are called as allocation. Sales distribution must be able to turned sales target into actual sales.

&mdash; Sales allocation is often done in monthly basis and it will be aligned with production planning.
