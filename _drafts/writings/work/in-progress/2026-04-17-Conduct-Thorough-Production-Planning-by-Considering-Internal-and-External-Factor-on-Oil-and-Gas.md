# The Importance of Production Planning on Manufacturing Industry

In the context of manufacturing industry, production planning is a process to plan production resource to met the sales allocation. For example, a tire producer allocation 100K pair of their shoes to customer A and the rest (200K) will be evenly distributed to other customer, then a production plan will be created in order to realize the demand. Those prodution plan will consist of following details

1. Master production schedule (MPS) - what to be produced, how much to be produced, when to be produced.
1. Material requirement planning
1. Bill of Material
1. Routings
1. Production Work order - Initially created during production planning phase but will be detailed out during production scheduling.

note that production planning is often done in yearly basis, while the production schedule is done in weekly/monthly or even day to day basis. Production scheduling will consider more granular factor such as maintenance, resource readiness, weather (if applicable) because production planning is done for long term and production scheduling is done in short term.
