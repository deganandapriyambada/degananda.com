# Difference between Production Planning and Production Allocation

Production planning mainly focus on the production target of finished goods. While production allocation is part of the production scheduling process to determine the actual resources assignment for the production execution process including actual schedule, operators, site, machine, number of produced goods and so on.

## The Importance of Production Allocation

Failure production allocation might implicate the whole production and operations sustainability. For example, if the production target is not match with the machine readiness due to the maintenance process, it could lead into production delay and will ended up to disspoint their customer because the delivery was delayed.

Another example is: if the raw material delivery schedule (good issue and good receipt schedule) is not aligned with the actual production resource assignment, it could ended up with all the resources (including labor and machine) to be idle (underutilized) or overutilized.

Hence, Production allocation play an important role to balance and optimize the sales allocation, production plan as well as the operational readiness.

There are three main target of production allocation:

1. Resource and capacity management
2. Demand matching between sales, production, operations and maintenance
3. Inventory and cost optimization

&mdash; Those three pillar ideally must be balanced out.
