# Production Allocation is Different with Production Scheduling

Production schedule is done once production plan especially once the MPS (master production schedule) finalized by productions. Sales allocation will be turned into actual production realization plan long term during production planning process, then the MPS will be converted into **indicative long term production work order**. 

Production schedule process will be focusing on converting long term production  work order into **short term and more detailed production work order** usually in weekly/daily or even hourly basis. Once the schedule is fixed (when the production will be executed), then resource(operator, equipment, factory, plant, etc) will be assigned. Those process are called as **production allocation**.

In context of material planning, production planning is responsible to create high level material requirement planning (MRP). Then, production schedule process will convert those MRP into GI/GR schedule/timeline and finally the material will be allocated in hourly based from the warehouse or within the plant storage itself.
