# Accounting Process Definition in Context of Oil and Gas

Accounting is formed from "account" noun which indicates historical debit or credit records over specific person or entity (eg: organization, company, etc). Credit means, the account got additional balances and debit means the balance is reduced.

Account balance can be a currency or anything which represents the amount of value possessed by an account. In context of banking, account balance means the avaiable money who held the by the account, while on oil and gas upstream context, account balance is referring to the amount of gas allocated for each stakeholder and quantified using physical volume units. Each stakeholder will have certain portion of well/reservoir ownership  represented in physical volume unit (eg: MMBTu/million british thermal units, scF/standard cubic feet or tonnes).

When more than one stakeholder is involved on the upstream facilities, it is essential to track the actual amount of each product variants (LPG, dry gas, condensate, natural gas, etc) produced by the facilities for each stakeholder. The product might be processed by the same gas pipeline, but the stakeholder need to know the **allocated volumes for their account**.

## Hydrocarbon Accounting System

MMBTu (million british thermal units) is unit of energy specifically used in oil and gas upstream LNG. 

	1 MMBTu is equal with 1K cubic feet of natural gas.

Hydrocarbon accounting system is a tools used to **measure**, **track** and **reconcile** the amount of gas (represented in MMBTu) which allocated to each stakeholder. It tracks the gas production volumes from wellhead until the LNG is allocated and loaded into vessel (depend on the contract).

Hydrocarbon accounting and lifting management are different although both of them are related to track and allocate hydrocarbon volumes for each stackholder. Lifting management is focused on the cargo deliveries (loading) **planning & scheduling**  of the hydrocarbon products once the production is done, while hydrocarbon accounting is there to measure, track and allocate hydrocarbon volumes through mass balances method.

Differences between Hydroncarbon Accounting, Lifting management and Mass Balances

| Metrics   | Hydrocarbon Accounting                                       | Lifting Management                                           | Mass Balances                                                |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Objective | Track, measure, monitor, reconcile and allocate hydrocarbon product for each stakeholder across all value chain | Plan, schedule and execute the cargo delivery process (arrival, deperature, berthing, etc) | a principle/methodology to ensure the amount of material leaving the production system is equal with the amount of material entering the production system |
| Output    | Allocated volumes, balance, reconciliation document          | Cargon schedule, lifting records                             | Verified mass balance closure.                               |
| Scope     | All process chain: processing, storage and export            | Cargo operations at terminal                                 | n/a (a principle/method) to check hydrocarbon imbalances/balance in specific system |

These are synergies: hydrocarbon accounting are based on mass balance principle to reconcile and determine  hydrocarbon product allocation for each stakeholder and then the allocated hydrocarbon product will be delivered to the stakeholder vessel through lifting operation management system (LOMS)
